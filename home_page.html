Jellyfish

What is a jellyfish?

A jellyfish is a free-swimming marine coelenterate (a group of aquatic, or marine organisms and a member of the Animal kingdom) with a gelatinous bell - or saucer-shaped body that is typically transparent and has stinging tentacles around the edge. Although being a small animal, many contain poison in their tentacles.
