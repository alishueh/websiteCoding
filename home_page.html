What is a jellyfish?

[Please view on 110% zoom] A jellyfish is a free-swimming marine coelenterate (a group of aquatic, or marine organisms and a member of the Animal kingdom) with a gelatinous bell - or saucer-shaped body that is typically transparent and has stinging tentacles around the edge. Although being a small animal, many contain poison in their tentacles.

What does the jellyfish symbolise?

A jellyfish symbolises beauty, wake-up calls, being in flow, regeneration, immortality, and intuition.
